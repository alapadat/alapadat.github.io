Interact With a Video

YouTube offers features that help you interact with a video better. You can Play/Pause a video, turn Subtitles and Autoplay on and off, and more. YouTube's features do a good job of giving you control without overwhelming you.

  • Pause/Play
    Take a break from content by pausing it. Get back in the action by pressing play.
  • Skip
    If you want to watch the next video in a playlist or queue instead of the video you are currently watching, Skip it.
  • Toggle Autoplay
    You may want the subsequent video to play after the current video. You may wish to make sure this doesn't happen. If you're playing music, you want to keep the party going. If you're discussing an episode of a documentary for a science class, you want to keep the class focused.
  • Fast Forward and Rewind
    If you're looking for information/missed something, you can fast forward or rewind the video to find it.
  • Subtitles
    Use Subtitles if the video is in a language you have trouble understanding, you are hearing-impaired, etc.
  • Picture in Picture
    With Picture in Picture, you can keep watching a YouTube video without being in the tab that's playing the video.
  • Mini Player
    If you want to browse YouTube while watching a video, use the Mini Player.
  • Theater Mode
    Theater mode makes the video window larger, but you can still like, comment, and browse other videos.
  • Full Screen
    Full screen sizes the video to fit your screen.
  • Desktop Shortcuts
    If you use YouTube a lot, shortcuts save a lot of time.
  • Like a Video
    You can express your opinion on a video by a video.
